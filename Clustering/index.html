💡 Theory

K-Means partitions n points into k clusters by alternating:

1. Assign each point to its nearest centroid.
2. Update centroids as the mean of assigned points.

Convergence is guaranteed but may be a local minimum.
K-Means++ selects initial centroids with probability proportional to d², giving dramatically better results than random initialization.

Agglomerative clustering starts with each point as its own cluster, then greedily merges the two closest, recording heights to build a dendrogram. Cut at height h → clusters existing before any merge at h or above.
